The Asian squat is a deep squat performed by people living in Asian countries for both practical and cultural reasons.


People from Asian countries are raised to sit in this highly comfortable position for resting, or in substitute for either standing or sitting in a chair.

Additionally, public restrooms have pans versus toilets, which require people to squat down. Having thigh-and-toilet-seat contact is seen as less sanitary than performing a deep squat in Asian countries.

How todo theAsian Squat

step 1-3

  1. Stand slightly wider than shoulder-width. Flare your toes out slightly.

  2. Crack at your hips and knees at the same time, and begin lowering yourself to the floor.

    3. Think about keeping your body-weight over the midline of the foot to ensure you’re not rocking forward or backward.

  3. Squat as deep as you can, while trying to keep your torso relatively upright and your heels on the ground. Rest your arms on your knees.

    4. Hold in this position for several minutes and try to feel your muscles ‘relax’.

Was it hard?

If yes , you're probably facing one of the following:

  1. You don’t have the right limb length proportions.

    2. Longer upper thigh bone than lower leg bone makes it harder. So does long legs combined with short torso.

  2. You don’t have the adequate mobility

    3. The Asian squat requires a superior level of mobility in the hips and ankles in order to perform correctly.

  3. You haven’t practiced the squat enough

    4. If you’re able to squat deep enough, but you just don’t have the stamina to hold the position, then it’s simply due to the fact that you haven’t practiced the position enough..
